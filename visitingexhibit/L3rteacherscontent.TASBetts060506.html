Recreating the Big Bang

Seminar Description
The big questions in science-such as "What is the origin of our Universe?"-are deceptively simple. The answers involve a multitude of interconnected ideas, some simple and some revolutionary. This seminar will follow the trail of questions and ideas that have led to our current view of the moment of creation-The Big Bang-and discuss recent laboratory tests of these ideas.

About the Instructor
Russell Betts is the Vice Provost for Planning and Programs and holds the position of Professor of Physics at the University of Illinois at Chicago. His work is well known in the study of cluster structure in atomic nuclei and he is the discoverer of several important phenomena in this field. As spokesman of the APEX experiment, he led the U.S. effort to resolve one of the most tantalizing problems of atomic and nuclear physics - the well-known "Positron Peak Problem." Most recently, he has led the UIC group in studies of high energy density QCD matter at Brookhaven National Laboratory's Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and at CERN's Large Hadron Collider.

"Doing History" in the Classroom:
 Rethinking the Introductory U.S. History Course

Interpretive Essays

Program Description: Research on "best practices" shows that students learn more from active, participatory lessons than from listening to lectures. Students are more likely to "own" the subject matter when they do hands-on investigation, use historical sources, and present their findings to peers and teachers. In other words, students learn more when they "do history" for themselves. The National History Project summer institute will focus on bringing these best practices into the introductory U.S. History course. A group of 15 history faculty from high schools and colleges will come together to "do history" for themselves and enrich their teaching strategies.

Without Santuary: Lynching in American History and Culture

Seminar Description
In the years following emancipation a wave of lynchings swept through the United States, especially in the southern states where slavery had been most prevalent. This seminar will investigate the causes of the post-emancipation rise of this form of racial violence as well as the history and social practices of lynching in the United States. The centerpiece of the seminar will be a tour of the Chicago Historical Society's "Without Sanctuary" exhibit of lynching photography, which the exhibit's curator will lead. We will also view source materials in the Newberry Library's Special Collections that relate to the history of lynching and discuss how to prepare and present this information for precollegiate audiences.

About the Instructor
Natasha B. Barnes is Associate Professor of English and African American Studies at the University of Illinois at Chicago

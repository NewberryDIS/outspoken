Ex-Office Boy Now Assistant to General Manager

Mr. Hugh Percival Walden purchasing agent at Chicago and Pullman Car Works for the last 16 years, has been appointed assistant to Vice-Pres. And Gen. Mgr. L.S. Hungerford, the position vacated by Mr. Frank L. Wood when he became assistant general manager with headquarters at San Francisco. Mr. Walden assumed his new position on May 7, and on the same date Mr. F.O. Reemer, assistant director of purchases, assumed charge of the purchasing for the manufacturing department.

Mr. Walden’s entire business experience has been with the Pullman Company, he having been employed as office boy on June 21, 1882, so he will have had 41 years’ continuous service this month. He was born Oct. 14, 1867, at Plumstead, Kent, England, and finished his schooling at St. Louis when his parents removed there. It was in the division superintendent’s office in St. Louis that he entered Pullman service and at that time Vice-Pres. Richmond Dean was chief clerk. In one year’s time Mr. Walden became a clerk, learned stenography, in May, 1885, was transferred to the district superintendent’s office, and in March, 1887, came to the Chicago offices following his chief, Mr. T. H. Wickes, when the latter was made general superintendent. From 1887 to 1905 Mr. Walden was secretary to Mr. Wickes, who by that time was vice-president and general manager. For the next two years he was assistant purchasing agent, and from March, 1907, to May 7 of this year purchasing agent with headquarters at the Pullman Car Works, handling the buying for the manufacturing department.

Mr. Reemer is a native Chicagoan and was educated in grammar school and business college. He began work in 1902 as office boy for the American Car & Foundry Company and later became assistant to the supply agent in Chicago. In 1912 he became purchasing agent for the Haskell & Barker Car Company, and in 1918-19 was its resident representative at Washington, D.C. In 1922 when the Pullman Company absorbed the Haskell & Barker company Mr. Reemer was made assistant director of purchases.

Professional Development Programs for Teachers

The Newberry Library offers a wide range of professional development programs for Chicago area teachers. Our content-based seminars bring distinguished scholars to the Library to share their work with participating teachers, as well as introducing teachers to the research possibilities in the Newberry's collections. The Newberry Library's programs for teachers are fully accredited by the Illinois State Board of Education, and those who participate earn recertification credits. The Newberry Library's Professional Development Programs for Teachers are supported by generous grants from the Field Foundation and the Chicago Community Trust.


The Chicago History Project (CHP) is a collaboration between Chicago Public Schools (CPS), the Newberry Library, the Chicago Historical Society, the Chicago Metro History Education Center, the Constitutional Rights Foundation Chicago, and the University of Illinois at Chicago. Funded through the Department of Education's Teaching American History program, CHP provides professional development for CPS teachers of American History working in grades 7-12. For more information, visit the Chicago History Project Web site at www.chicagohistoryproject.org, e-mail chp@newberry.org, or call Frank Valadez at (312) 255-3569.

Chicago Teachers as Scholars (TAS) is a partnership between Chicago Public Schools, the Newberry Library, and the University of Illinois at Chicago. TAS offers a series of two-day seminars on topics as diverse as Mexican culture, women in medieval Europe, the Harlem Renaissance, and evolutionary biology to CPS teachers. This program is open to all CPS teachers. For more information, e-mail teacherprograms@newberry.org, or call Ginger Shulick at (312) 255-3524. TAS is generously funded by the Polk Bros. Foundation, the Lloyd A. Fry Foundation, The Albert Pick, Jr. Fund, Peoples Energy, The State Farm Foundation, and McDougal Littell.

The Newberry Teachers' Consortium (NTC) offers Chicago-area teachers a series of one-day seminars in six AP-related subject areas: American History; European History; Library Science; Political Science, Economics, and Governement; Literature and Drama; and World History and Geography. Each group meets at the Newberry Library with a distinguished scholar three or four times during the school year to discuss topics suggested by NTC members. Advance registration and a membership fee are required. For more information, e-mail teacherprograms@newberry.org, or call Victoria Murphy at (312) 255-3714.

Classics in Context is run through the Chicago Humanities Festival and offers a series of one-day seminars designed to allow educators to re-examine popular and widely-taught works. The seminars take place at several academic facilities; this year the Newberry Library will host a discussion of Miguel de Cervantes' Don Quixote on January 22, 2005. For more information, visit the Chicago Humanities Festival Web site at www.chfestival.org, or if you would like to attend a seminar, contact Greg Alcock at greg@chfestival.org.

Historic Maps in K-12 Classrooms is a resource designed to bring historically significant map documents into your classroom. Historical background and commentary on the map, supplemental images and text, student exercises, and lesson plans created for different grade levels (K-2, 3-5, 6-8, and 9-12) accompany each map. Designed to accommodate a variety of K-12 curricula, this collection of materials exploits the particular ability of historic maps to excite students' imagination of past landscapes, events, and human geographical conditions.

The Newberry Library Seminars offer stimlating and affordable classes designed to encourage discussion in the humanities. These seminars are open to the general public, and several courses are offered for CEU credit. Unlike the Teachers as Scholars seminars, participants in Newberry Library Seminars must pay tuition. Substitute coverage is not provided, but many courses meet on weekday evenings or on Saturdays. For more information please call the Newberry Library's Center for Public Programs at 312-255-3700.

THE NATIONAL HISTORY PROJECT
SUMMER INSTITUTE FOR TEACHING FACULTY

Doing History in the Classroom:  Rethinking the Introductory U.S. History Course

June 25 to July 19, 2002
Welcome to the National History Project summer institute at the Newberry Library.  The institute is designed around the concept of "doing history," that is, developing questions about the past, researching in archives and libraries, and sharing interpretations with peers.  Participants will have ample opportunity to conduct research at the Newberry or other Chicago archives.  Each participant, including the institute leaders, will produce an interpretive essay tied to documents.  We will edit and evaluate these documents as a group, and publish these essays and sources on the Newberry's Web site.  We will also keep journals documenting our experiences in the institute.

The Schedule:

Seminars focus on assigned readings (both scholarly historical interpretations and primary sources).  See the listing for each seminar for the reading assignment.  Some of these sessions are led by guest scholars.
(* indicates that a reading is available on reserve.)

Workshops and Group Meetings will focus on developing and carrying out research projects.

The Historians Chair is a concept adapted from the National Writing Project’s writer’s chair.  As in the writing project, the Historians Chair places each participant in front of the group to present a coherent piece of work—lesson plan, interpretation, or primary source analysis. 

The Editorial Board is a fancy way of saying we will all sit around a table and offer constructive criticism on one another's work.  The idea here is to review participants’ interpretive essays as a group of peers who are producing a common publication.

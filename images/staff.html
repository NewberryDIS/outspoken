Nisan Chavkin is the Associate Director for the Consitutional Rights Foundation Chicago. He has designed and directed seminars, institutes, and extended programs of study for secondary level teachers on a variety of topics, including law in American history, equal protection, federalism, service learning, the First Amendment, voting rights, and the American jury system. He holds a B.A. cum laude in Jewish Thought and History of the Jewish People from the Hebrew University of Jerusalem.

Robin Fleming is Program Coordinator for Grants and Contracts with the Chicago Public Schools Office of Professional Development. She has extensive experience coordinating professional development workshops for school principals, administrators, and teachers with CPS and the North Central Regional Educational Laboratory. She holds a B.A. from Northwestern University in Political Science.

Toby Higbie (CHP Academic Director). Toby Higbie is the Director of the Dr. William M. Scholl Center for Family and Community History at the Newberry Library. He was Project Director for the Work and Community History Workshop, a workshop for school teachers and librarians supported by a National Endowment for the Humanities Focus Grant. He is also Chicago Coordinator for the National History Project, a joint program of the Woodrow Wilson National Fellowship Foundation and the Newberry Library to create a research-oriented professional development project for high school and college faculty. Dr. Higbie is a historian with expertise in the area of U.S. social and cultural history, and is the author of Indispensable Outcasts: Hobo Workers and Community in the American Midwest, 1880-1930 (University of Illinois Press, 2003).

Robert D. Johnston is a Professor of History and the Director of Teacher Education in the History Department at the University of Illinois, Chicago. Dr. Johnston received his Ph.D from Rutgers University and is the author of The Radical Middle Class: Populist Democracy and the Question of Capitalism in Progressive Era Portland, Oregon (Princeton University Press, 2003).

Jen Koslow is the Assistant Director of the Dr. William M. Scholl Center for Family and Community History at the Newberry Library. Dr. Koslow received her Ph.D. from the University of California at Los Angeles where she wrote a dissertation on the development of urban health care systems. She has created instructional Web sites at UCLA and at the Newberry Library, and has taught U.S. history at the university level.

Barbara McCarry, O.S.B., is the Special Projects Manager for the Chicago Public Schools, which includes curriculum writing, instructional delivery to teachers, and networks with the many institutions/museums in Chicago. She is also the Chicago Coordinator for the Gilder Lehrman Institute programs. McCarry has taught elementery and high school History and English, and was an elementary school principal for fourteen years.

Heidi J. Moisan is a Public Historian for Teacher and Student Programs at the Chicago Historical Society where she has worked for approximately eight years. As part of her responsibilities, Heidi researches, writes, and develops exhibition related curriculum materials and professional development courses for educators. She has an M.A. and B.A. in History from Northern Illinois University in DeKalb, IL. Heidi received the Illinois Association of Museums Superior Achievement Award for Excellence in Curriculum Materials in 1995. She has been a presenter at several conferences, including the 2001 and 2002 Tribune Teacher Resource Fair; the 2000 TechEd Chicago: Teaching and Learning in a Network World; the 1999 Chicago Public Schools Cultural Enrichment Initiative; and the 2003 and 1999 Council for American Studies Education (CASE).

Lisa Oppenheim is the Associate Director of the Chicago Metro History Education Center.

Ann Marie Ryan is currently the Associate Director of the Teaching of History Program, at the University of Illinois at Chicago, which prepares undergraduate and graduate level students to teach history in secondary schools. Also at UIC, she served as a program associate on the Big City Teacher Preparation Initiative funded by the MacArthur Foundation in the College of Education. Prior to her work at UIC, Ann Marie was a senior program associate with Facing History and Ourselves in Chicago. She has also served as a secondary school administrator and history teacher at Eastside Catholic High School in Bellevue, Washington. She is currently working on her Ph.D. in Curriculum and Instruction at the University of Illinois at Chicago, has an M.A. in Curriculum and Instruction from Seattle University and a B.A. in History from Loyola University of Chicago.

Ginger Shulick  is the Project Assistant for the Dr. William M. Scholl Center for Family and Community History at the Newberry Library.

Frank Valadez (CHP Project Director). Frank Valadez is the Newberry Library's Director of Professional Development Programs for Teachers. He was formerly an editor at McDougal Littell, where he worked in the development of history textbooks. He has an A.M. in U.S. History from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.

From the antislavery groups of the 1840s to the "poetry slam" movement of the present, Chicago has always been a vibrant locale for free speech activism.  

Drawing on the combined collections of the Newberry Library and the Chicago Historical Society, Outspoken will provide a rare opportunity to discover the history of political, cultural, and artistic dissent in Chicago and its hinterlands.
